Location: Mitchondrial Matrix for Eukaryotes

Step 1: pyruvate's carboxyl group (COO-) is completely oxidized and given off as a molecule of CO2. This is the first step that results in the release of CO2.
The remaining 2-carbon fragment undergoes an oxidizing reaction as their electrons are transferred to NAD+, reducing it to NADH.
Finally, coenzyme A (CoA), a sulfur-containing compound which comes from Vitamin B5, is attached to the two-carbon intermediate, forming Acetyl-CoA.
This came directly from pyruvate oxidation. Acetyl-CoA adds its acetyl group (2-carbon fragment) to oxaloacetate, initiating the cycle. This forms citrate, a 6-carbon molecule.
