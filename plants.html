Photosynthesis

Overview

Light Reaction

  • Occurs in the thylakoid inside the chloroplast
  • Uses solar energy to make ATP AND NADPH

Dark Reaction

  • Occurs in the stroma inside the chloroplast
  • Uses the ATP and NADPH produced in the previous reaction to incorporate CO2molecules into organic molecules, forming G3P (sugar).

Stage One: Light Reaction

Location: Thylakoids

  1. Photon of light strikes one of the pigment molecules in a light harvesting complex in PS II, exciting an electron to a higher energy level.
  2. This electron is captured by primary electron acceptor and is transporting through the ETC.
  3. A water molecule is split into 2 electrons with their H+ by an enzyme, and the oxygen atom combines instantly with another to produce the byproduct, O2.
  4. The photoexcited electron travels from PS II to PS I, establishing the proton gradient (higher inside thylakoid space).
  5. In PS I, another photon of light excites the electron to a higher eneergy level, captured by ferodoxin (Fd) then transferred to NADP+, making NADPH.
  6. This process continues as light keeps on exciting electrons donated by H2O.
